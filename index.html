Bio

I was born in Ferrol in 2001. My purpose is to understand how brain dynamics support movement and cognition, with a particular focus on the cerebellum. I take a translational view of this work: I want to help improve the quality of life of people with neuropsychiatric impairments while uncovering the fundamental computations the brain performs. I am also committed to bridging the gap between academia and the market.

I am a Clarendon Scholar pursuing a PhD in Systems Neuroscience at the University of Oxford, where my research investigates cerebro-cerebellar dynamics using OPM-MEG, supervised by Caroline Nettekoven, Mark Woolrich, and Laurence Hunt. I hold an MSc in Neuroscience from Oxford, supported by a la Caixa Foundation fellowship.

Before Oxford, I trained in medicine. I have been awarded several competitive research scholarships, including selection as the only European student in the Harvard Stem Cell Institute Internship Program (2022) and a Ministry of Education research scholarship for my medical thesis, and I co-authored research papers during medical school. My long-term goal is to become a physician–scientist and to lead my own research group.

Beyond science, I love spending time in Galicia — my home — surrounded by family and friends. I find calm in observing nature and in practising sport.
